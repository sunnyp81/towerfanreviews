Dimplex is a brand that most British homeowners will recognise, even if they have not specifically bought a fan from them. The brand has been part of UK homes for decades, primarily through its electric heating products, and its expansion into tower fans is a natural step for a company that understands air comfort appliances.

## Who Dimplex is

Dimplex is a British brand with a long history in electric heating, dating back to the 1970s. Today the brand is part of the Glen Dimplex Group, an Irish-owned multinational. Despite the corporate structure, Dimplex maintains a distinctly UK-focused product range and a high street retail presence that many newer brands lack entirely.

This heritage matters in practical terms. Buying a Dimplex fan means buying from a brand with established UK customer support, accessible warranty processes and in-store availability at major retailers. For buyers who prefer to see a product before purchasing, or who want easy recourse if something goes wrong, that is a genuine advantage.

## What Dimplex fans are known for

Dimplex fans are workhorse appliances: practical, unpretentious and built to last through several seasons of regular use. The build quality reflects the brand's heating appliance heritage, with sturdy construction and reliable controls. There is nothing unnecessarily complicated about a Dimplex fan, which suits buyers who want to switch it on, set their preferred speed and get on with their day.

The heater-fan crossover is a genuine distinguishing feature. Given that UK summers are short and winters are long, a fan that also provides useful heating during the colder months offers better year-round value than a cooling-only model. Dimplex brings real expertise to the heating side of this, which is more than can be said for some brands that add heating as an afterthought.

Retail availability is another practical strength. Dimplex fans appear in Currys, Argos and similar stores, which means you can physically compare them before buying and handle returns in person if needed. This is genuinely useful for buyers who are less comfortable with online-only purchasing.

## Limitations to consider

Dimplex fans are not leading the market on smart features. App control, voice assistant integration and precise speed calibration are not priorities for this brand. If you want a fan that integrates with Alexa or Google Home and can be scheduled from your phone, you will find the Dimplex range limited by comparison to Dreo, Levoit or Dyson.

The styling is functional rather than design-led. Dimplex fans look appropriate in a practical setting but would not be the first choice for buyers prioritising aesthetics in a living room or bedroom. This is a question of preference rather than a fault.

The noise profile at higher settings is unremarkable by mid-range standards. Dimplex fans are not particularly loud, but they are also not specifically tuned for quiet operation in the way that the Honeywell QuietSet or Dreo Pilot Max are.

## Choosing within the Dimplex range

The two models in our comparison above represent the main options currently available. The DXMBCF is the more compact and straightforward cooling fan, suited to standard room cooling use. The Mont Blanc model offers a step up in finish and, depending on the specific variant, may include additional features or a wider oscillation arc.

If year-round use matters, lean toward whichever Dimplex model includes heating functionality. If you want a simple, reliable summer fan and nothing more, either model does the job dependably.

## Value verdict

Dimplex is the right choice for buyers who value brand familiarity, in-store availability and no-nonsense reliability over cutting-edge features. The price sits in the mid-range, and the product delivers what the price suggests: a solid, practical tower fan from a brand you can trust. It is not trying to out-engineer Dyson or out-feature Dreo, and it does not need to.
