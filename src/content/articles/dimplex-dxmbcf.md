The Dimplex DXMBCF is the brand's entry-level cooling tower fan, sitting at around £50 and aimed squarely at buyers who want a recognisable brand without paying premium prices. Dimplex has a long history in home heating and cooling, and the DXMBCF carries that workmanlike reputation into budget territory. The question is whether it does enough to justify choosing it over cheaper white-label alternatives.

## Design and build quality

The DXMBCF is a fairly compact tower fan by modern standards, standing at around 82 cm. The black finish suits most rooms without drawing attention, and the overall construction feels solid rather than flimsy. It is not the most visually striking fan on the market, but it is neat and unobtrusive.

The controls are straightforward: a button on the unit cycles through the three speed settings and toggles oscillation on or off. There is no display to speak of, just a couple of indicator lights. That simplicity works in its favour if you want a fan you can operate without reading the manual, though it does mean you have to walk over to it every time you want to change the speed.

The oscillation mechanism runs smoothly and quietly, which is not always the case on budget fans where the gears can rattle.

## Airflow and cooling

Three speeds is a common setup at this price, and the DXMBCF delivers what you would expect: a gentle breeze on low, a moderate draught on medium and a reasonably strong airflow on high. It is best suited to personal cooling, such as a desk, a reading chair or a bedside table, rather than circulating air across an entire room.

On the top setting the airflow is satisfying for direct use, reaching a comfortable distance of a metre or so. Spread it across a larger living room via oscillation, though, and the effect becomes subtle. If you are trying to cool a warm bedroom before sleep, point it towards you rather than relying on oscillation alone.

## Noise

This is where the DXMBCF does reasonably well. On its lowest speed it is genuinely quiet, with a smooth tone that blends into background noise. The middle speed remains acceptable. The top speed introduces a more audible motor note, but it is not significantly louder than other fans in this category.

For desk use or light background cooling in a bedroom, the lower two speeds are practical. You would not use the top speed for sleeping unless you are a heavy sleeper or it is particularly hot.

## Running costs

Tower fans are cheap to run, and the DXMBCF is no exception. Drawing a modest amount of power even on its highest setting, running it overnight would add only a few pence to your electricity bill at typical 2026 UK rates. There is no need to be cautious about leaving it on for extended periods. For a more detailed breakdown, see our [tower fan running cost guide](/tower-fan-running-cost/).

## Features

The DXMBCF keeps things minimal. You get three speeds, oscillation, and nothing else. There is no remote, no timer, no sleep mode and no ioniser. For many buyers that is perfectly fine, particularly if this is going in a spare room or used sporadically during a heatwave. But if you want the fan to switch off while you sleep or control it from across the room, you will need to look elsewhere.

## Is the Dimplex DXMBCF worth it?

The DXMBCF is a reliable, no-nonsense tower fan that does what it says. Dimplex's build quality is a step above anonymous budget brands, and the quieter low setting makes it a reasonable bedroom option. The lack of any features beyond oscillation is the main limitation, and at £50 you are paying partly for the brand name rather than additional functionality.

If your needs are simple, it earns its place. If you want a remote or a timer, spending a little more on the [Honeywell HY254E QuietSet](/honeywell-hy254e-quietset/) makes more sense.
